1.1 Introduction
The modernization of the agricultural sector is a strategic priority for improving market
transparency and strengthening national food security.
This first chapterconstitutes the preliminary phase in the development of the
AgriGov Marketplatform, a digital ecosystem designed to transition the Algerian agri-
cultural sector from traditional, fragmented trade to a centralized digital marketplace.
Currently, the sector faces critical challenges, including market monopolies, inefficient in-
teractions between buyers and sellers, and significant logistics barriers that often lead to
product spoilage. By bridging the gap between production, logistics, and regulation, our
objective is to implement a national platform that directly connects farmers, buyers, and
transporters while allowing the Ministry to oversee quality standards and official pricing.
A precise understanding of these requirements is essential to design a solution that ensures
user accountability, optimizes goods movement, and provides a reliable hub for all.
To achieve this, the chapter is structured to provide a clear path from the initial
problem analysis to the final requirements synthesis. We begin with a comparative study
of existing systems to identify their limitations and better position our solution. We then
define the various human and system actors interacting with the platform, followed by
the identification of functional requirements through detailed Use Case diagrams. And
we present the Textual Descriptions and the System Sequence Diagrams (SSD) to model
the dynamic interactions between the actors and the system. Next we showcase the user
experience through the Human-Machine Interfaces (HMI), presenting web and mobile
prototypes. Finally, the chapter concludes by summarizing the identified needs, which
will serve as the architectural foundation for the design and implementation phases of the
project.

Identification of Limitations
The analysis reveals that while commercial platforms likeEasyplantsandAgrimpexcel
in the purchasing process, they leave significant gaps in the agricultural supply chain:
•Specialized Logistics:None of the systems provide a dedicated portal for inde-
pendent transporters to manage missions in real-time.
•Inventory for Producers:Retail-focused systems like Easyplants do not offer
inventory management tools specifically for farmers or producers.
•State Regulation:There is a lack of tools for governmental bodies to set and
enforce price authorities to prevent market monopolies.
Project Positioning
AgriGov Marketbridges these gaps by combining robust e-commerce features with
a professional logistics engine and administrative oversight tools, creating a complete
ecosystem for the Algerian agricultural sector.

system. It shows the interactions between external actors (users or other systems) and
the specific actions (use cases) the system performs. Its main purpose is to define the
scope of the system and "who" can do "what" without explaining the technical details.
